_Orchestration is the automated configuring, coordinating, and managing of computer systems and software. It's about bringing together disparate things into a coherent whole. In the classical sense, you’ve got strings, brass, woodwinds and percussion, all with their own parts, with a conductor at the helm to ensure consistency and timing so as to produce harmony instead of cacophony..._

One way in which I would describe myself...

An experienced *Information Technology* professional who prefers common terminology over domain specific distinction. Which is to say I'm stubborn when it comes to defining myself with any sort of language that implies anything in particular.  Driven with an obsession for learning, heavily influenced by minimalist approach to software and systems, I'm often distracted with thoughts on how better to improve the user workflow experience.

In addition to these habits, I also carry a deep passion for music composition and soundscape design. Part of my practice in software development uses live coding as a way to sharpen improvisational and creative logic...

When not engaged otherwise, I like to have fun with the philosophical applications of language in our stories depicted in media, in order to better understand the process of crafting applications using Large Language Models.
