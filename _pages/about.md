A self-taught computational scientist who's definitely taken the scenic route. The variant, sometimes chaotic nature of my experience gives me a strong appreciation for structure and syntax, whether it's in the rules of language or the architecture of a software system. I do my best to bring a thoughtful, often wry perspective to the big questions surrounding technology and its impact on how we see ourselves. Having both observed and particpated in the rise and (sometimes) fall of grand technological aspirations, I believe in a healthy dose of skepticism tempered with a genuine fascination for what's possible. and I'm always up for a conversation that digs beneath the surface, which is to say, I can be easily distracted by any thoughtful idea.

When I'm not wrestling with systems and code or pondering the delightful notions Existenial chores, I mostly enjoy thinking about and spending time with my partner – we're both pretty keen enthusiasts of life in general. My own biases are no grander, and my capacity for affection no less, than anyone else who can appreciate the subtle nuances of someone who, as a rule, puts others first with a kind of pragmatic compassion that would make the Dalai Lama seem like a bit of a jerk.
