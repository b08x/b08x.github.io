Pragmatic Eclecticism

Exploring the selective and utility-driven framework for constructing a cohesive "Architecture of Meaning" that defines human existence and change. This approach prioritizes what works—combining ideas from conflicting domains to achieve philosophical utility, scientific rigor, and functional application.

Foundational Eclecticism

This section explores the fundamental negotiation of the human condition: the tension between predetermined form and radical freedom. The framework pragmatically selects two opposing linguistic theories, not as a contradiction, but as the essential poles of our existence. Click on the cards to see how each theory contributes to the model.

The "Given" (Form)

Theory: Universal Grammar (UG)

UG is chosen to model the innate, biological blueprint of language. It represents the "unchosen, given architecture" that provides a universal starting point and defines our shared humanity.

The "Chosen" (Freedom)

Theory: Systemic Functional Linguistics (SFL)

SFL is chosen because it views language as a network of conscious, contextual choices. It models our radical freedom and the individual's responsibility to create authentic meaning.

Reconciliation via Existentialism

The framework utilizes Existential Philosophy to reframe the tension between UG's determinism and SFL's radical choice. This tension is not a contradiction, but a reflection of the fundamental negotiation inherent in being human. The grammar we use becomes the very medium through which we execute this constant negotiation and articulate our existence.

Mechanistic Eclecticism

This layer of the framework selects specific neuroscientific and speculative concepts to provide tangible, biological, and theoretical proof for the subjective phenomena of meaning and choice. These selections are prioritized to ground the framework's philosophical claims in objective correlates.

Shared Reality

Mechanism: Interpersonal Neural Synchrony (INS)

INS, the coupling of brain activity between people, is chosen as the observable, biological proof of a shared social reality. It serves as the neurobiological indicator that the existential negotiation has succeeded in creating a shared mental world.

Radical Change

Mechanism: Psychedelic-induced Neuroplasticity

This is selected as the definitive biological model for profound psychological reorganization. By disrupting the Default Mode Network (DMN), it provides a mechanism for breaking rigid thought patterns and actively reconstructing subjective reality.

Subjectivity & Choice

Mechanism: Quantum Cognition

Chosen as a speculative tool to address phenomena defying classical logic. It provides a "theoretical space" for human subjectivity and a new conceptual language for agency, incorporating fundamental randomness to model non-deterministic free will.

This clinical application demonstrates a powerful synthesis of the eclectic approach. It constructs a multifactorial model to explain systemic relational fracture in an AuDHD dyad by integrating components from several distinct disciplines. Role of Quantum Cognition

Quantum Cognition is a key speculative component, selected pragmatically to ensure the framework is theoretically complete. It addresses the aspects of human experience that classical, deterministic views cannot fully explain. Its purpose is not to be an empirically proven fact, but to provide the necessary conceptual space to account for subjectivity and free will.

Rationale: Why It's Necessary

Classical models are insufficient to explain the dynamic, often illogical, nature of human decision-making. Quantum cognition is utilized because it can model "quantum-like" behaviors and account for the fundamental indeterminacy of choice, which classical probability fails to capture.

Function: What It Provides

  • Origin of Subjectivity: Provides a theoretical home for the nuances of subjective experience central to existential thought.
  • Language for Agency: Offers a new vocabulary to describe the indeterminate nature of free will.
  • A Conceptual Bridge: Acts as a potential link between the objective brain and subjective awareness, tackling the "hard problem" of consciousness.
