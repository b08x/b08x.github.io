06:59:35 PM-assistant

The frequency_penalty and presence_penalty are parameters that can be used when generating prompts with the OpenAI API. They can affect the testing of pragmatic adherence in the following ways:

  • frequency_penalty: This parameter penalizes new tokens based on their frequency in the dataset. A high frequency_penalty (close to 1) makes the model less likely to use common tokens, while a low frequency_penalty (close to 0) makes the model more likely to use common tokens. If you're testing for the presence of certain common informal words in the generated prompts, a high frequency_penalty might make these words less likely to appear, potentially making the prompts appear more formal.

  • presence_penalty: This parameter penalizes new tokens based on whether they appear in the text so far. A high presence_penalty (close to 1) makes the model less likely to repeat itself, while a low presence_penalty (close to 0) makes the model more likely to repeat itself. If you're testing for the overuse of certain words or phrases, a high presence_penalty might make these words or phrases less likely to be repeated, potentially making the prompts more varied and interesting.

Here's how you could adjust the Ansible task to use these parameters:
