Combining Functional, Generative, and Pragmatic Grammar for Enhanced Natural Language Processing Applications

Abstract

This article presents a novel approach to correlating functional and generative grammar with ideal pragmatic grammar. The proposed approach leverages the strengths of each grammar type to create a comprehensive and practical framework for natural language processing (NLP) tasks.

Functional grammar focuses on the syntactic structure and relationships within sentences, while generative grammar emphasizes the rules and processes for generating grammatically correct sentences. Ideal pragmatic grammar, on the other hand, considers the context and communicative intent behind language use.

By combining these three perspectives, the proposed framework provides a more holistic understanding of language and enables more accurate and effective NLP applications. The framework can be applied to a wide range of NLP tasks, including machine translation, text summarization, and dialogue generation.

The article presents a detailed description of the proposed framework, along with experimental results demonstrating its effectiveness on various NLP tasks. The results show that the framework outperforms existing approaches in terms of accuracy, fluency, and coherence.

The proposed framework has the potential to significantly advance the field of NLP and open up new possibilities for human-computer interaction.
