> In the software development industry, there is a growing population of programmers, with figures increasing at an exponential rate. However, this exponential growth means that half of all programmers have less than five years of experience. Despite this inexperience, many programmers are pressured to deliver code quickly, leading to the production of low-quality code. In order to write good code, programmers must take their time to study the problem thoroughly and move deliberately, rather than rushing to meet deadlines. Poorly written code can lead to a variety of issues, including rigidity and fragility, resulting in a codebase that is difficult to understand, maintain, and modify. By taking the time to write good code, programmers can ultimately work more efficiently and produce higher-quality software.
