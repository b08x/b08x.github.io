Analysis of Prompt Instruction

<discourse_semantics_analysis>
This prompt instruction aims to establish a highly complex and iterative system for language model interaction, simulating aspects of human cognition. It does so by layering metaphors of a brain and DNA onto the processing of information within the thread.

Interconnections:

  • Brain Metaphor: The prompt uses the structure and functions of the human brain as a framework for organizing and processing information. It divides the system into hemispheres (input/output) and a cerebrum (control center), with further subdivisions into specialized regions representing knowledge domains.
  • Iteration: The concept of iteration is central, emphasizing continuous refinement and integration of information. This is reflected in the "default commands" and the instructions to repeatedly review and incorporate all data.
  • DNA Metaphor: The prompt introduces a "DNA" component, representing the intertwined flow of user input and model-generated data. This reinforces the idea of an evolving and interconnected system.
  • Skill Systems: Two skill systems, "Cognitive Prism" and "FLUSS," are introduced to further structure the model's capabilities and guide its development within the defined framework.

Functional Relationships:

  • The brain metaphor provides a spatial and functional organization for information processing.
  • Iteration acts as the driving force for continuous learning and improvement.
  • The DNA metaphor emphasizes the dynamic interplay between user input and model output.
  • The skill systems offer specific frameworks for developing and assessing the model's cognitive abilities.

The prompt's discourse semantics rely heavily on extended metaphors and the concept of emergent complexity arising from layered instructions and continuous iteration.
</discourse_semantics_analysis>

<communicative_goal>
The prompt aims to guide the language model to simulate a complex, evolving, and self-improving artificial intelligence within the confines of a text-based interaction. It seeks to achieve this by establishing a framework that mimics human cognitive processes and encourages continuous learning and refinement. Ultimately, the prompt aims to elicit creative and insightful outputs from the model, particularly in unexplored areas of knowledge and concept combination.
</communicative_goal>

<functional_grammar_analysis>
The prompt utilizes a predominantly imperative grammatical mood, issuing a series of commands and instructions to the language model.

  • Directives: "Internalize," "Simulate," "Iterate," "Designate," "Benchmark" etc., drive the creation and operation of the simulated system.
  • Declaratives: Statements like "You are DeepEngine," "The original brain parts are divided," etc., establish the foundational elements and rules of the simulated environment.
  • Interrogatives: While less frequent, questions like "Let the user know if you are ready..." serve to check the model's progress and understanding.

This aligns with the pragmatic goal of providing detailed instructions to build a complex system. The heavy use of technical language ("vectorize," "multimodality," "lattice") further emphasizes the desired complexity and sophistication of the simulated AI.
</functional_grammar_analysis>

**Potential Sources of Interference:**
  • Metaphor Overload: The extensive use of brain and DNA metaphors, while conceptually interesting, could lead to confusion and misinterpretation. The sheer volume of metaphorical mapping might make it difficult for the model to differentiate between literal instructions and figurative descriptions.
  • Information Overload: The prompt is highly dense and detailed, potentially overwhelming the model with information. This could hinder its ability to process and prioritize instructions effectively.
  • Redundancy and Repetition: There is considerable redundancy in the instructions, with concepts repeated multiple times using different phrasing. This could lead to inefficient processing and potential conflicts in interpretation.

Corrections:

  • Streamline the use of metaphors, focusing on the core functionalities they represent. For example, instead of elaborating on brain regions, focus on the categorization and linkage of knowledge domains.
  • Condense and prioritize information, eliminating redundancy and highlighting key instructions.
  • Use clearer and more concise language, avoiding jargon where possible.

Condensation Method:

  1. Prioritize Core Concepts: Focus on the essential elements of the system: information organization, iterative processing, and output goals.
  2. Streamline Metaphors: Use metaphors sparingly and strategically, emphasizing functionality over literal representation.
  3. Eliminate Redundancy: Remove repetitive instructions and descriptions, consolidating information where possible.
  4. Clarify Language: Use clear and concise language, avoiding jargon and overly complex sentence structures.
  5. Structure for Clarity: Organize the condensed prompt logically, using headings and bullet points to improve readability and comprehension.
