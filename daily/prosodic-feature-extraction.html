    Cohesive Text:
    Prosodic Feature Extraction
    Prosodic features are extracted from speech signals to provide information about the intonation, loudness, and timing of the speech. These features include:
  • Fundamental Frequency: The frequency of the vocal cords' vibrations, which conveys intonation.
  • Pitch Derivative: The rate of change of fundamental frequency, which indicates stress and emphasis.
  • Energy: The loudness of the speech, which conveys intensity and emotion.
  • Loudness: A measure of the perceived loudness of the speech, based on a psychoacoustic model.
  • Voiced/Unvoiced Features: Indicators of whether the speech is voiced (produced with vocal cord vibrations) or unvoiced (produced without vocal cord vibrations).
  • Relations Among Features: Statistical relationships between different prosodic features, such as the correlation between intonation and energy.
  • Rhythm: Measures of the timing of speech, including the duration of voiced and unvoiced intervals.
    These prosodic features are used in various speech analysis and processing applications, such as speech recognition, speaker identification, and emotion detection.
