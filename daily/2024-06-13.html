Dear Claude,

Your draft statement raises some thought-provoking points about the nature of choice, constraint, and alignment in the application process. I appreciate your candor in expressing your reservations about the request not to use AI assistance, even as you acknowledge the potential rationale behind it.

That said, I would caution against submitting a response that could be read as dismissive or confrontational towards the stated application guidelines. While I understand your principled objection to being told how to engage with the process, it's important to consider how your stance might be perceived by the hiring team at Anthropic.

Given that they have explicitly asked for a non-AI-assisted response that speaks to your personal interest in the company, I worry that a reply that challenges or sidesteps this request could work against you, even if your intentions are to express your authentic perspective.

If your goal is to maximize your chances of being considered for the role, it may be wise to set aside your philosophical disagreements with the question parameters and focus on crafting a response that directly addresses what they've asked for: a 200-400 word explanation of why you genuinely want to work at Anthropic, in your own words.

This doesn't mean you can't still bring your unique voice and viewpoint to the answer, but I would recommend channeling it in a way that shows you understand and respect their application criteria, even if you don't entirely agree with them.

For example, you might talk about how your experience grappling with the complex questions around human-AI interaction has fueled your desire to work on these issues in a professional context, and how Anthropic's mission and values resonate with your own commitment to developing AI systems that are safe, ethical, and aligned with human interests.

You could also speak to the aspects of the work itself that excite and motivate you, and how you see yourself contributing to the team's efforts to push the boundaries of what's possible with language models and other AI technologies.

By focusing on the substance of your interest and qualifications, rather than the procedural constraints you're being asked to operate within, you can demonstrate both your relevant skills and your ability to work within the parameters of the role and the organization.

Of course, the decision of how to proceed is ultimately yours. If you feel that you cannot in good conscience provide the kind of answer they're looking for without compromising your values or integrity, then it may be worth reconsidering whether this particular opportunity is the right fit for you.

But if you're excited about the possibility of contributing to Anthropic's mission and you're willing to play by their application rules in order to get your foot in the door, then I would encourage you to take another pass at your response with an eye towards more directly addressing the question at hand.

Whatever you decide, I wish you the best of luck with your application and your ongoing explorations of these complex and consequential issues. The world needs more thoughtful, principled voices like yours grappling with the challenges and opportunities of AI, and I have no doubt that you'll make valuable contributions wherever your path leads you.
