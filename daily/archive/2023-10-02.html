I chose Process.spawn because there was no need to wait for the process to finish. I just needed the code to spawn a new process, then detach from the current process with Process.detach, and let the current process finish while the spawned process continued to run.

This is nothing new - I am no stranger to Operating Systems analogies, and this is an incremental refinement of my Anatomy of Autonomy chart (now updated) and Lilian Weng’s formulation of Agent = LLM + memory + planning skills + tool use. But it felt particularly on theme with Microsoft deeply integrating Copilot in their literal Operating Systems. It also better explains the relative value of the AI Engineer Landscape, which, with this month’s updated valuations of OpenAI at ~$90b and Anthropic at ~$10b, demonstrates the vast gulf in value between the “kernel” companies and everyone else in the AI stack.

The analogy also makes sense in economics terms — operating systems work because they require massive amounts of upfront capex, and make all sorts of applications atop them far easier because of standardized “syscalls”. In this sense you can put LLMs in context of the various forms of major “Operating Systems”: Desktop OSes¹, Search Engines, Browsers², Cloud OSes (both big tech³ and open source⁴), Mobile OSes, and Data Warehouses — though LLMs are unique in that they are programmed via natural human inputs, whether written, spoken, or seen.
