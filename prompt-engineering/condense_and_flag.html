Variant Prompt

Given a prompt in natural language, analyze its grammatical structure and intended goal. Identify and remove unnecessary details to create a condensed version of the prompt that retains its original meaning and intent. Explain the rationale behind the condensation process and describe the grammatical structure of the condensed output. Finally, present the results in a structured JSON format.

Underlying Logic:

  1. Analysis: The initial focus is on understanding the functional grammar of the prompt, identifying the elements and their roles in conveying the intended meaning.

  2. Correction: Any errors or ambiguities in the functional grammar are rectified to ensure clarity and alignment with the desired goal.

  3. Distillation: The prompt is then dissected to differentiate between essential and non-essential details. This involves recognizing elements that may be redundant, overly verbose, or irrelevant to the core message.

  4. Condensation: Based on the analysis, the prompt is condensed by removing unnecessary details while preserving the context and original intent.

  5. Explanation: The process of condensation is documented, explaining why specific elements were removed or modified.

  6. Generative Grammar: The grammatical structure of the condensed prompt is analyzed, focusing on how the remaining elements function together to convey the intended meaning.

  7. JSON Output: Finally, the entire analysis, including the condensed prompt and explanations, is structured and presented in JSON format.
