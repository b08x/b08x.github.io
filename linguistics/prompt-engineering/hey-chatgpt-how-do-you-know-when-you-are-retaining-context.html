Hey chatGPT: how Do You Know when You Are Retaining Context?

Source: tree of thoughts

As an AI, I keep track of the ongoing conversation by using the input from the current conversation thread. This includes the original prompt as well as the subsequent interactions. I use this information to form responses that are contextually relevant and consistent with the previous dialogue. When I indicate with the 🧠 emoji, it means that the current response has taken into account the context from the preceding discussion.

However, it's important to note that my ability to retain context has limitations. For instance, I don't have the capability to remember or learn from past interactions beyond the current conversation. Also, very long conversations might exceed my context window (the amount of text that I can consider at one time), which could lead to loss of some context. That's why it's often useful to periodically summarize or restate the context in long conversations.

I can understand code snippets, explain what they do, and help debug simple problems across a variety of languages. However, when it comes to writing or generating code, I can produce simple snippets across various languages, but the complexity and accuracy of the code can vary. I am often best at generating Python code, given its popularity in the training data and its broad use in the data science and AI communities.
