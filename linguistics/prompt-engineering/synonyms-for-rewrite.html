Synonyms for rewrite

Emphasize Verb 1. Give special importance or prominence to (something) in speaking or writing. Highlight certain points more than others. Imperative mood
Diversify Verb 1. Make or become more diverse or varied. Add variety, perhaps in sentence structure or vocabulary. Declarative mood
Neutralize Verb 1. Render (something) ineffective or harmless by applying an opposite force or effect. Remove bias or opinion, making the text more objective. Declarative mood
Streamline Verb 1. Design or provide with a form that presents very little resistance to a flow of air or water, increasing speed and ease of movement. 2. Make (an organization or system) more efficient and effective by employing faster or simpler working methods. Remove unnecessary content or fluff. Imperative mood
Enrich Verb 1. Improve or enhance the quality or value of. 2. Make (someone) wealthy or wealthier. Add more pizzazz or detail to the rewrite. Imperative mood
Illustrate Verb 1. Provide (a book, newspaper, etc.) with pictures. 2. Explain or make (something) clear by using examples, charts, pictures, etc. Provide examples to better explain the point. Declarative mood
Synthesize Verb 1. Make (something) by synthesis, especially chemically. 2. Produce (sound) electronically. Combine different pieces of information. Declarative mood
Sensationalize Verb 1. Present information about (something) in a way that provokes public interest and excitement, at the expense of accuracy. Make the rewrite more dramatic. Imperative mood
Humanize Verb 1. Make (something) more humane or civilized. 2. Give (something) a human character. Make the text more relatable or personal. or lift (something) up to a higher position. 2. Raise to a more important or impressive level. Declarative mood
Illuminate Verb 1. Light up. 2. Help to clarify or explain (a subject or matter). Prompt for a rewrite that is crystal-clear or enlightening. Declarative mood
Enliven Verb 1. Make (something) more entertaining, interesting, or appealing. Means make the text more lively or interesting. Imperative mood
Soft-pedal Verb 1. Play down the importance or scale of (something). 2. Moderate or restrain the tone or level of. Means to downplay or reduce the intensity of the text. Imperative mood
Exaggerate Verb 1. Represent (something) as being larger, better, or worse than it really is. When you want to hype-up hyperbole in the rewrite. Great for sales pitches (just watch those pesky facts)! Imperative mood
Downplay Verb 1. Make (something) appear less important than it really is. 2. Treat or speak of (something) so as to emphasize its unimportance. When you want a more mellow, mild-mannered tone. Great for research, and no-nonsense evidence-based testimonials. Declarative mood
Glamorize Verb 1. Make (something) seem glamorous or desirable, especially undeservedly so. Prompt to make the rewrite sexier and more appealing. Imperative mood
