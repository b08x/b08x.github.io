Transformational-Generative Grammar (TGG)

"Does the sentence sound right?"

It is also known as Transformational Grammar. TGG is a part of the theory of generative grammar. Rules that attempt to determine how a sentences is to be generated...

Generative grammar is a linguistic theory that uses rules to assign structural descriptions to sentences. For example,a generative rule might state that a verb must be followed by a subject. This rule would be used to generate sentences like “John runs quickly,” “John runs slowly,” and “John runs to the store”.

Noam Chomsky, the founder of generative grammar, created the phrase "Colorless green ideas sleep furiously" as an example of generative grammar. While the words are understood, the actual content of the sentence is nonsensical. However, an English speaker can still understand the thing being described. This sentence has become the standard by which his theory has been evaluated.

source

Generative grammar seeks to extract rules that generate sentences. Transformational grammar accounts for the relationships among of the various elements of a sentence.

There are four types of transformation rules: deletion, insertion, substitution, and movement. - Substitution is a technique used to determine an expression that can be substituted using another expression in phrases or sentences. For example, “John speaks clearer than you” can be substituted with “John speaks more clearly”.

Misunderstanding Ai

Using a term such as "transformation" may give the impression that theories of transformational generative grammar are intended as a model of the processes by which the human mind constructs and understands sentences, but Chomsky clearly stated that a generative grammar models only the knowledge that underlies the human ability to speak and understand, arguing that because most of that knowledge is innate, a baby can have a large body of knowledge about the structure of language in general and so need to learn only the idiosyncratic features of the language(s) to which it is exposed. source
